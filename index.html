On Getting Here

The one who started out being an ass. And stayed anyway.

❦

It has been almost a year since you said "heya, wassup" like it was nothing — July 25th, 2025, though I didn't know yet that it would turn into the only conversation I never wanted to end. It wasn't smooth from there. There were stretches of five days without a text, both of us too proud to be the one who reached first. And then, somehow, without either of us deciding it out loud, five days became every day became every hour, became you asking me, on the 27th of May, if I'd be yours properly. I said yes before you'd finished the sentence.

You were, by your own admission, an absolute ass about the whole thing at first — all deflection and bad timing, like you needed convincing that this was allowed to be easy. I still don't know how you did it: staying steady through the version of me that was nothing but a mess to deal with, never once panicking, never once making me feel like too much. Nobody warns you that the calm ones are the ones worth keeping.

"I love you" used to feel like enough. Somewhere around month eleven it started feeling like the smallest, most inadequate three words for the size of this thing.

I can roast you into the ground and you come back with the exact same energy instead of sulking about it, and that might be the thing I trust most — that we can be ridiculous with each other and it never once tips into cruel. This page won't say it better than a conversation would. Scroll back to the beginning if you want. Watch it start over. That's sort of the whole point of us, anyway — we keep starting over, every single day, on purpose.
